Xiang An (Chinese: 安翔) is a Research Scientist and Team Lead of the Multimodal Large Model Group at GlintLab. He specializes in computer vision and multimodal large models.

His current research focuses on building the next-generation Vision Transformer (ViT) to address urgent needs in modern MLLMs. He is also the #2 contributor to the InsightFace ecosystem (~27k⭐).

Research

Xiang An leads the Multimodal Large Model Group at GlintLab, where his team is building the next-generation Vision Transformer (ViT) to address some of the most urgent needs in modern MLLMs.

Research Interests

  • Vision Transformers (ViT) - Developing efficient visual encoders for multimodal models
  • Large Multimodal Models - Building comprehensive vision-language systems
  • Face Recognition - Large-scale training methods and datasets
  • Visual Representation Learning - Universal and compact image representations

Open Source

Xiang An has made significant contributions to the open source community. Key projects include:

InsightFace ⭐ 27k+

#2 contributor to the state-of-the-art 2D & 3D face analysis library. Contributions include Glint360K dataset, Partial FC training method, and arcface_torch framework.

LLaVA-OneVision-1.5 ⭐ 651

Team Leader. A fully open framework designed to democratize multimodal training. Released mid-training and instruct data for community use.

UNICOM ⭐ 699

Lead Author. Universal and compact representation learning framework for image retrieval (ICLR 2023, ECCV 2024, ICCV 2025 Highlight).

LLaVA-NeXT ⭐ 4.4k

Vision module contributor. Enhanced OCR capabilities and optimized the visual encoder for text-rich and document images.
